## Backstory

*“It’s no surprise that I am as in tune with death as I am. It’s always been there with me and always attracted to me. “*

I was born to a poor couple who maintained sizable farmland to feed the entire nearby village. I don’t remember much from then as I was young but I’ve been told they were good honest workers. The two were wildly efficient at their work and quite underappreciated in the community. They managed to produce a variety of crops year after year, season after season to sustain the entire village. You may be asking, “Why were they poor if they had that much harvest and fed that many people?” They were too nice and would give food to everyone even if everyone couldn’t pay my parents. The money they did make was spent to maintain our equipment and take care of me. But they were passionate about their work and loved what they did with their heart. They believed they were doing good by feeding the village and that they would be blessed when the time came.

I would love to say that blessing came but it never did.

When I was 2, a fire ravaged our house and consumed the farmland. I managed to survive and my first memory is the sight of the land burning to ash, the smell of smoke thick in the air, and the cold touch of my parent’s skeletons holding me as I watched the fire. When the fire died down, my skeletal parents carried me to the nearby village and left me at the doorstep of the town mayor. This village was small but frequent travelers had people in and out to keep things lively. There were always fresh faces in the taverns. The mayor reluctantly took me, having a girl and a boy of his own to take care of aged 6 and 3, respectively. Over time I began to assimilate into the family and was accepted as if I were their own. In no time, I was celebrating my 5th birthday with my adoptive family and some would say that I had found my home. Despite that, the memory of my home being burned brought me many nightmares and I felt regret that I had somehow survived and my parents had perished. I tried to occupy my thoughts and time with reading, playing games with my siblings, and getting up to general mischief around town; which is easier said than done when you are the mayor’s kids. I slowly began to grow closer to my brother, Klaus, who was only a year older than me. By the time Klaus was 10 and I was 9, we were near inseparable. Whenever our parents would separate us for any reason, big or small, we would throw a fit. Things were starting to feel better. I felt happy and at home. That’s until the plague hit our village. It took the lives of most of the village and left my parents in critical condition. And as if things weren’t bad enough with my parents hanging on by a thread, a group of the undead attacked the shambles of my village and kidnapped Klaus and me.

We spend what felt like an eternity in the back of a pitch black carriage on a bumpy path to god knows where. The carriage came to an abrupt halt and someone pulled back the covers of the carriage. The light blinding us as a silhouette formed in the light. “Get out you snot nosed brats” said the figure. Klaus and I stumbled out of the wagon. The air was stiff and the sky full of endless clouds with no sun in sight. I looked at him and realized we had both been crying the entire ride but were silent as to be strong for the other. The figure that yelled at us turned out to be an undead that attacked our village. He shoved Klaus and I towards a weather-worn and beaten castle. It looked older than anything or anyone I had met yet here it stood; absolute. We navigated through the corridors and halls occasionally being shoved from behind by the undead, eventually reaching the door to what seemed to be a grand hall. The door was flanked by undead soldiers holding spears. As we approached they broke their formation and opened the doors for us. We entered into a dark room save for a single ray of light raining down on the throne at the other end of the hall. In the throne sat a man, paper thin with eyes devoid of any emotion. His robes were so black it seemed that the light that touched it simply disappeared as if it never existed. The undead continued to shove us until we were standing at the foot of the raised platform the throne sat upon. When we reached that spot, all the undead in the room and the ones escorting us immediately dropped to their knees. The man on the throne says, “You raid an entire village and bring me these two pathetic excuses. Was there no one else worthy?” I glanced around and the undead remained motionless. He continues, “No matter. What are your names?” “Mmmm-yy name is Ka-ka Kadulus and this is my brother, Klaus” I say in a shaky voice. He lets out a deep throaty croak as if to acknowledge our names and brotherhood. He stands from the throne, stumbles a bit, and steadies himself. He descends from the platform to examine Klaus and I. He circles us for a bit as if to see if he can divine something. He lays his hand on my shoulder and I feel something almost like lightning crawl down my spine and I hear a hissing voice in my head, “Join him. With his power and knowledge, you will be unstoppable”. The man removes his hand from my shoulder and ascends back up to the throne and sits. He sighs heavily and says, “I see great potential in you both but it will require some training. You shall both become my apprentices in Necromancy” Klaus begins to open his mouth to respond but I quickly put a hand out to stop him. I say, “We would be honored to learn from you” and give an encouraging nod to Klaus. The following morning, we began our training with him and what came to be the most harrowing 10 years of our lives.

We trained in everything. Hour upon hour upon hours of strength workouts, necromancy lessons, reading, studying, library time. We barely had time to breath, let alone sleep or eat. The days seemed to crawl almost as if time had stood still. Klaus and I struggled for a long time; often unable to sleep due to our ache bodies or our splitting headaches. Finally, the eve of the lunar eclipse came and our master had readied us for this moment. He said to us, “On this night, during the lunar eclipse, I will imbue you with some of my power and solidify all that I have taught you over these many years.” At this point we were about 8 years through our training with what felt like no end in sight. Klaus, 18, and myself, 17, we knelt at the foot of the throne listening to our master ramble on. The next day, the eclipse came and our master, us and the undead performed the ritual atop the castle’s highest tower. As the eclipse reached its peak, our master completed his spell, the circles of undead around us began to dissolve into dust which is getting vacuumed in the eclipse above us and almost as if he knew that it would come down, our master stepped back from us as a two bolts of lightning came down and struck us. These bolts consume all the light around them as if creating a void of light and upon being struck, Klaus and I fell to the ground writhing in pain and vomiting up our stomach. After a few moments of the most excruciating pain imaginable, we stumbled to our feet. I could feel this new magical power surging through me. Our master dropped a set of bones at each of our feet and said, “Now raise them”. Instinctually, we both waved our hands and the skeletons began to form into a being. We both looked at each other shocked as we had practiced the motion many times but nothing ever happened. Our master spoke, “Your connection to shadowfell is complete. Over the next couple of years, your connection will grow stronger until you have become one with it.”
